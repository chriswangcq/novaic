# Active non-test media-byte surface classification is not complete

## Summary

`R714` is useful and mostly accurate, but it does not yet earn success under the stricter one-go bar. The result classified the main active Runtime/Cortex shell/display paths and identified likely legacy/standalone surfaces, but it still leaves production media-byte surfaces unresolved rather than fully classified. A follow-up is needed to close the residual classification gaps before `P732` can be marked successful.

## Blocking Gaps

- The result did not classify every production hit from the scan. For example, `novaic-mcp-vmuse/src/novaic_mcp_vmuse/tools/windows.py` imports `base64` but was not inspected or classified.
- The Device VmControl screenshot endpoint in `novaic-device/device/vmcontrol_routes.py` was marked as a possible remediation candidate, but the classification did not determine whether it is still an active product-facing path, a legacy compatibility route, or safe to retire.
- The standalone `novaic-mcp-vmuse` HTTP/MCP byte surfaces were grouped broadly. That is not enough for the stated success criterion that non-test emission paths be listed and classified with file pointers.
- Because the ticket was one-go, these residual uncertainties cannot be waived as non-blocking.
