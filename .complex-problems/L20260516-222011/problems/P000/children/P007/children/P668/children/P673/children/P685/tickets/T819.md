# Align topology docs, runbooks, and CI guards with service topology evidence

## Problem Definition

Service topology was classified in P684 (8 services, specific ports, specific entrypoints). Docs, runbooks, and CI guards must be compared against this evidence and patched where stale.

Target surfaces:
1. **Architecture docs**: service-topology.md, runtime-architecture.md, cortex docs, and other port-referencing docs.
2. **Runbooks**: cloud-production.md, local-backends.md, local-dev.md.
3. **CI guards**: 30+ lint scripts in scripts/ci/ that may reference service boundaries, retired paths, or topology assumptions.

## Proposed Solution

Split into:
1. Architecture docs alignment — compare service descriptions against classification evidence.
2. Runbook alignment — compare operational instructions against service topology.
3. CI guard alignment — verify guards match current topology and are not stale.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Docs/runbooks that describe worker/service topology are located and compared.
- Low-risk stale descriptions are patched.
- Deferred surfaces are recorded as residual risk.
- Relevant doc lints and CI guards pass.

## Verification Plan

- Read each doc/runbook referencing ports or services.
- Run CI guard scripts that check topology/boundary claims.
- Verify no lint regression.

## Risks

- Patching docs could introduce new inaccuracies if the classification evidence is wrong.

## Assumptions

- P684 classification (8 services, specific ports, entrypoints) is accurate.
